About Cruzeiro Esporte Clube

Discover the rich history and identity of our beloved club

Our History

Cruzeiro Esporte Clube was founded on January 2, 1921, originally as Palestra Itália by the Italian community in Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, Brazil. The club was established with the purpose of promoting football and bringing together the Italian immigrant community.

The Name Change

In 1942, during World War II, the club changed its name from Palestra Itália to Cruzeiro Esporte Clube. This transformation reflected the political climate of the time and marked a new era in the club's history. The new name, inspired by the Southern Cross constellation (Cruzeiro do Sul), became a symbol of Brazilian identity and pride.

Club Identity

Nickname: "A Raposa" (The Fox) - representing cunning, intelligence, and agility on the field.

Colors: Blue and White - representing the sky and purity, reflecting the club's noble aspirations.

Stadium: Mineirão (officially Estádio Governador Magalhães Pinto) - one of the most iconic venues in South American football.

City: Belo Horizonte, located in Minas Gerais, the heart of Brazil's mining region.

Golden Era

The 1970s and 1990s marked Cruzeiro's most successful periods. The club won the Copa Libertadores in 1976 and 1997, establishing itself as a continental powerhouse. During the 1990s and 2000s, Cruzeiro became synonymous with excellence, winning multiple domestic titles and becoming a breeding ground for world-class talent.

Legacy and Impact

Cruzeiro has produced some of the greatest players in world football history, including Ronaldo, Rivaldo, Dida, and many others. The club's commitment to developing young talent through its academy has made it one of the most respected institutions in Brazilian football. Today, Cruzeiro continues to be a symbol of passion, resilience, and excellence in the sport.
